Understanding the world

Old blood.
Modern power.

The year is 2030. Werewolves make up roughly twenty percent of the global population, but their existence remains concealed from ordinary human society through secrecy, political agreements, and institutions built to keep both worlds apart.

Their hidden society is divided among four ruling Houses: powerful bloodlines that govern separate territories, control major institutions, and preserve laws older than anyone still remembers.

They no longer rule from castles. Their influence moves through corporations, hospitals, universities, private estates, and guarded roads. Their authority changed its clothes; the instincts beneath it did not.

History survives in fragments. Law preserves rituals whose original purpose has been forgotten. Bloodlines still carry gifts their heirs were never taught to recognise.

Origins · Religion · Forgotten history

The moon remembers
what law forgot.

The laws governing werewolf society began as sacred rites. Centuries of political ambition slowly turned belief into regulation, tradition into obligation, and history into something carefully edited by those in power.

The lore archive follows Lunaerie’s first blessing, the rise of the ruling bloodlines, the Covenant of Eight, the fall of the Alpha Kings, and the forgotten True Luna families whose inheritance still moves quietly through the modern world.

Begin here to understand why the Houses rule, what their laws once meant, and which parts of the official history were deliberately buried.
